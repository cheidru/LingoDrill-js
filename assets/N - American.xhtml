Newbie - American?

Ben is being introduced to Teacher Li. But what’s this about fruit?

měi guó rén?

美国人？



Teacher Zhang: Teacher Li, this is Ben, my student.
zhāng lǎoshī: lǐ lǎoshī, zhè shì běn, wǒ de xuésheng.

张老师: 李老师, 这是本, 我的学生。

Ben: Hello! My name is Ben, I’m American (someone without fruit).
běn: nǐhǎo, wǒ jiào běn, wǒ shì měi guó rén (méi guǒ rén).

本: 你好! 我叫本, 我是美国人 （没果人）。

Teacher Li: Hello, I have fruit, I’m “someone with fruit”.
lǐ lǎoshī: nǐhǎo, wǒ yǒu guǒ, wǒ shì "yǒu guǒ rén".

李老师: 你好, 我有果, 我是 "有果人"。

Ben: You have fruit?
běn: yǒu guǒ?

本: 果人?

Teacher Li: Didn’t you say “You are someone without fruit”, that you don’t have fruit? I have fruit, I am “someone with fruit”.
lǐ lǎoshī: nǐ búshi shuō"nǐ shi méi guǒ rén", nǐ méi yǒu guǒ ma? wǒ yǒu guǒ, wǒ shi "yǒu guǒ rén".

李老师: 你不是说 "你是没果人", 你没有果吗？ 我有果, 我是 "有果人"。

Ben: No, what I said was "American".
běn: bú shi, wǒ shuō de shì "měi guó rén".

本: 不是, 我说的是 "美国人"。

Teacher Li: That’s right. "American", "someone without fruit". In Chinese, if the tone is not the same, then the meaning is also not the same.
lǐ lǎoshī: zhè jiù duì le. "měi guó rén", "méi guǒ rén", zhōngwén lǐ, shēngdiào bùyīyàng, yìsi yě bùyīyàng.

张老师: 这就对了。 "美国人", "没果人", 中文里, 声调不一样, 意思也不一样。

Ben: Ok, I get it. Thank you, Teacher Li!
běn: hǎo de, wǒ zhīdàole. lǐ lǎoshī, xièxie nǐ!

本: 好的, 我知道了。 李老师, 谢谢你!

Words

学生 [xuésheng] - student

就 [jiù] - at once / right away / only / just (emphasis)

里 [lǐ] - inside / internal / interior

声调 [shēngdiào] - tone / note / a tone (on a Chinese syllable)

不一样 [bùyīyàng] - different / distinctive / unlike

意思 [yìsi] - idea / opinion / meaning

知道了 [zhīdàole] - OK! / Got it!

谢谢 [xièxie] - thank you
